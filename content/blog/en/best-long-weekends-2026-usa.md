## Your 2026 Long Weekend Map

2026 is a strong year for long weekends. Several federal holidays fall on Thursdays and Mondays, creating natural bridge opportunities. Here's every one worth knowing about.

## January

**New Year's Day — Thursday, January 1**
Take Friday Jan 2 off → 4-day weekend (Thu–Sun). Start the year right.
PTO cost: 1 day. Days off: 4. Efficiency: 4.0x.

**Martin Luther King Jr. Day — Monday, January 19**
Already a 3-day weekend. Take Tue–Fri off for a full 9 days (Sat Jan 17 – Sun Jan 25).
PTO cost: 4 days. Days off: 9. Efficiency: 2.3x.

## February

**Presidents' Day — Monday, February 16**
3-day weekend built in. Extend backward: take Thu–Fri (Feb 12–13) off → 5 days total.
PTO cost: 2 days. Days off: 5. Efficiency: 2.5x.

## April

**Easter — Sunday, April 5**
Easter isn't a federal holiday, but Good Friday is observed in 11 states. If yours is one of them, take Mon–Thu after Easter off → 10 days (Fri Apr 3 – Sun Apr 12).
PTO cost: 4 days. Days off: 10. Efficiency: 2.5x.

Even without Good Friday, taking Mon–Fri off gives you 9 days for 5 PTO days.

## May

**Memorial Day — Monday, May 25**
The classic summer kickoff. Take Tue–Fri before (May 19–22) → 9 consecutive days.
PTO cost: 4 days. Days off: 9. Efficiency: 2.3x.

Or keep it simple: just take Friday May 22 off → 4 days (Fri–Mon).
PTO cost: 1 day. Days off: 4. Efficiency: 4.0x.

## July

**Independence Day — Saturday, July 4**
When July 4 falls on Saturday, most employers observe Friday July 3. Take Mon–Thu (Jun 29 – Jul 2) off → 9 days (Sat Jun 27 – Sun Jul 5).
PTO cost: 4 days. Days off: 9. Efficiency: 2.3x.

Budget option: Take just Thursday Jul 2 off → 4 days (Thu–Sun).
PTO cost: 1 day. Days off: 4. Efficiency: 4.0x.

## September

**Labor Day — Monday, September 7**
3-day weekend. Extend: take Tue–Fri before (Sep 1–4) → 9 days.
PTO cost: 4 days. Days off: 9. Efficiency: 2.3x.

This is the last long weekend of summer — prices for beach destinations start dropping the week after.

## October

**Columbus Day / Indigenous Peoples' Day — Monday, October 12**
Observed federally and in most states. Take Tue–Fri off → 9 days.
PTO cost: 4 days. Days off: 9. Efficiency: 2.3x.

October is shoulder season in Europe — flights and hotels drop significantly after summer.

## November

**Veterans Day — Wednesday, November 11**
Mid-week holiday. Take Thu–Fri off → 5 days (Wed–Sun).
PTO cost: 2 days. Days off: 5. Efficiency: 2.5x.

**Thanksgiving — Thursday, November 26**
Most workers get Thu–Fri off. Take Mon–Wed before → 9 days (Sat Nov 21 – Sun Nov 29).
PTO cost: 3 days. Days off: 9. Efficiency: 3.0x.

This is one of the best windows of the year if you can leave Saturday and return the following Sunday.

## December

**Christmas — Friday, December 25**
Take Dec 28–31 (Mon–Thu) → 10 days (Thu Dec 24 – Sun Jan 3).
PTO cost: 4 days. Days off: 10+. Efficiency: 2.5x.

If your employer gives Dec 24 as well: take just Dec 29–31 → 10 days for 3 PTO.
PTO cost: 3 days. Days off: 10. Efficiency: 3.3x.

## The Bottom Line

With 15 PTO days, a US worker can realistically get **35–40 total days off** in 2026 by stacking around holidays. That's more than double what most people achieve by taking random weeks off.

The trick is planning early. Every one of these windows works better when you book flights and hotels before prices spike.
