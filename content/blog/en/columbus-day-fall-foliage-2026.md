## The Forgotten Long Weekend

Columbus Day (or Indigenous Peoples' Day, depending on your state) is one of the most underused long weekends of the year. It falls on **Monday, October 12, 2026** — right in the heart of fall foliage season.

Many workers forget about it entirely. Some employers don't observe it. But if yours does, you have a free 3-day weekend sitting in the most beautiful month of the year.

![Columbus Day 2026 PTO calendar showing how 1 PTO day on Friday October 9 creates a 4-day weekend through Monday October 12](/blog/columbus-day-calendar.svg)

## The 1-Day Play: 4 Days Off

Take Friday, October 9 off:

| Date | Day | Status |
|------|-----|--------|
| Fri Oct 9 | Friday | **PTO day 1** (also Hangeul Day in Korea — fun fact) |
| Sat Oct 10 | Saturday | Weekend |
| Sun Oct 11 | Sunday | Weekend |
| Mon Oct 12 | Monday | Columbus Day / Indigenous Peoples' Day |

**1 PTO day → 4 days off. Efficiency: 4.0x.**

That's one of the highest single-day PTO returns of the year.

## The Full Week: 9 Days Off

Take Tuesday through Friday off the week after Columbus Day:

| Date | Day | Status |
|------|-----|--------|
| Fri Oct 9 | Friday | **PTO day 1** |
| Sat–Mon Oct 10–12 | | Weekend + Holiday |
| Tue Oct 13 | Tuesday | **PTO day 2** |
| Wed Oct 14 | Wednesday | **PTO day 3** |
| Thu Oct 15 | Thursday | **PTO day 4** |
| Fri Oct 16 | Friday | **PTO day 5** |
| Sat Oct 17 | Saturday | Weekend |
| Sun Oct 18 | Sunday | Weekend |

**5 PTO days → 10 days off.** Enough for a serious fall road trip or international getaway.

## The Best Fall Trips for a 4-Day Weekend

![Destination cards for Columbus Day fall trips: Vermont Route 100, Shenandoah, White Mountains NH, and Asheville NC](/blog/columbus-day-destinations.svg)

**Road trips (drivable from major cities):**

- **NYC → Vermont (Route 100)** — The most iconic fall drive in America. Start in Bennington, wind north through Weston, Killington, Stowe. 4 days is perfect.
- **DC → Shenandoah Skyline Drive** — 105 miles of ridge-top driving with pull-offs at every overlook. Pair with a night in Charlottesville.
- **Boston → White Mountains, NH** — Kancamagus Highway is the classic. October is the reason this road exists.
- **Chicago → Door County, WI** — Peninsula jutting into Lake Michigan. Orchards, lighthouses, fish boils, and blazing maples.

**Fly-to destinations:**

- **Asheville, NC** — Blue Ridge Parkway is peak foliage in mid-October. The food and brewery scene makes Asheville worth the trip even without leaves.
- **Portland, Maine** — Lobster, lighthouses, and gold-red forests. Flights from NYC are often under $150 round trip.
- **Bend, Oregon** — A different kind of fall: golden aspens against volcanic peaks. Craft beer capital of the Pacific Northwest.

## If Your Employer Doesn't Observe Columbus Day

About 30% of private employers don't give Columbus Day off. If that's you:

- **Take Monday as PTO instead** → Still a 4-day weekend for 2 PTO days (Fri + Mon)
- **Or pivot to Veterans Day** → November 11 is a Wednesday in 2026. Take Thu–Fri off for a 5-day break (2 PTO). Foliage has moved south by then — Smokies, Ozarks, and Texas Hill Country are peaking.

## October Is Cheap

October is shoulder season for most international destinations too:

- **Europe** — Summer prices are gone. Rome, Barcelona, and Lisbon are 60–75°F with 40% fewer tourists than summer.
- **Japan** — Fall foliage season starts in late October in Nikko and northern Honshu. Still pre-peak for Kyoto (which peaks in November).
- **New Zealand** — Spring has arrived. Shoulder season pricing, wildflowers blooming, hiking conditions ideal.
