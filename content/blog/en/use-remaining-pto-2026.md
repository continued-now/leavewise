## The $2,500 Mistake

According to the US Travel Association, 55% of American workers don't use all their vacation days. The average worker forfeits 4.6 days per year. At median compensation, that's roughly $2,500 you're donating back to your employer.

It's October. If you have unused PTO, the clock is ticking. But the good news: Q4 has some of the best vacation windows of the entire year.

## Your Remaining Days Playbook

### You Have 2 Days Left

**Best move: Thanksgiving extension**

Take Wednesday November 25 and the Tuesday before (November 24):

- Tue Nov 24 → **PTO**
- Wed Nov 25 → **PTO**
- Thu Nov 26 → Thanksgiving
- Fri Nov 27 → Day after (most employers)
- Sat–Sun Nov 28–29 → Weekend

**2 PTO days → 6 days off. Done.**

### You Have 3 Days Left

**Best move: Christmas to New Year's**

Take Monday December 29 through Wednesday December 31:

- Wed Dec 24 → Christmas Eve (many employers close)
- Thu Dec 25 → Christmas
- Fri–Sun Dec 26–28 → Weekend
- Mon Dec 29 → **PTO**
- Tue Dec 30 → **PTO**
- Wed Dec 31 → **PTO**
- Thu Jan 1 → New Year's Day
- Fri–Sun Jan 2–4 → Weekend

**3 PTO days → 11 days off (Dec 24–Jan 4). Efficiency: 3.7x.**

This is the single highest-efficiency window of the year. If you only have 3 days, use them here.

### You Have 5 Days Left

**Best move: Thanksgiving + Christmas**

Split them: 2 days for Thanksgiving (see above), 3 days for Christmas-to-New-Year's.

**5 PTO days → 17 days off across two separate breaks.**

### You Have 8+ Days Left

**Best move: Full Q4 optimization**

With 8 or more days, you can afford all of the above plus a mid-October or November escape:

- **Columbus Day week (Oct 12):** Take Tue–Fri off → 9 days (if your employer observes Columbus Day)
- **Random November week:** Early November is travel's best-kept secret. Flights and hotels are at yearly lows. Take a full Mon–Fri for 9 days, or stack it around Veterans Day (Nov 11, Wednesday) for a mid-week break.

## The "Use It or Lose It" Trap

Many companies have use-or-lose policies — unused PTO expires December 31. Some allow limited rollover (often 5 days max). Check your employee handbook for:

1. **Rollover cap:** How many days carry over?
2. **Blackout dates:** Some companies restrict PTO in late December for year-end close.
3. **Payout policy:** A few companies pay out unused days, but this is increasingly rare.

If your company has a **PTO use-it-or-lose-it policy with a December deadline**, don't wait until December to use them. November is far less stressful and cheaper for travel.

## Why You Should Plan Now, Not Later

Every week you wait in Q4, travel prices climb:

- **October bookings** for November travel: baseline pricing
- **November bookings** for December travel: +15–25%
- **December bookings** for same-month travel: +30–50%, limited availability

The difference between booking a Christmas trip in October versus December can be $300–$800 per person on flights alone.

## Quick Destinations for a November Escape

November is off-peak almost everywhere that isn't a ski resort:

- **Mexico City:** Perfect weather (65–75°F), cheap flights, incredible food scene. 4-day trip is ideal.
- **Sedona, AZ:** Fall colors, hiking weather in the 60s, no summer crowds.
- **Savannah, GA:** Spanish moss, historic architecture, mild temps. A long weekend here costs less than a nice dinner in NYC.
- **Puerto Rico:** Warm, no passport needed, and November is shoulder season between hurricane and high seasons.
