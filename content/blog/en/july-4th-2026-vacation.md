## Saturday July 4th — Better Than You Think

When Independence Day falls on a Saturday, it feels like a wasted holiday. But most federal employers and a majority of private companies observe the preceding Friday, July 3, as the holiday. That gives you a built-in 3-day weekend without touching your PTO.

The opportunity is in the days before it.

![Calendar showing the July 4th 2026 PTO window: 4 PTO days from June 29 to July 2 combined with the observed holiday and weekends for 9 days off](/blog/july-4th-calendar.svg)

## The 9-Day Strategy

Take Monday through Thursday off the week leading into July 4th:

| Date | Day | Status |
|------|-----|--------|
| Sat Jun 27 | Saturday | Weekend |
| Sun Jun 28 | Sunday | Weekend |
| Mon Jun 29 | Monday | **PTO day 1** |
| Tue Jun 30 | Tuesday | **PTO day 2** |
| Wed Jul 1 | Wednesday | **PTO day 3** |
| Thu Jul 2 | Thursday | **PTO day 4** |
| Fri Jul 3 | Friday | Independence Day observed |
| Sat Jul 4 | Saturday | Independence Day |
| Sun Jul 5 | Sunday | Weekend |

**4 PTO days → 9 days off. Efficiency: 2.3x.**

## The 1-Day Play

If you can only spare one day, take Thursday July 2 off:

**1 PTO day → 4 days off (Thu Jul 2 – Sun Jul 5). Efficiency: 4.0x.**

## Why Early July Is Great for Travel

July gets a bad reputation for being "peak everything." But early July — specifically the last week of June through July 5 — has some advantages:

**Domestic travel peaks July 4th weekend specifically.** Fly out Saturday June 27 and return Sunday July 5, and you avoid the worst of the holiday weekend airport crush. Most people are traveling Wednesday–Friday before the 4th and Sunday after.

**International destinations are in full summer mode but not yet at August peak.** European cities (Barcelona, Rome, Paris) are warm and lively without the August madness when all of Europe is on vacation simultaneously.

**National parks** are busy but manageable in late June. The true crush comes mid-July through August.

## Where to Go With 9 Days in Early July

![Destination cards for July 4th travel: National Parks road trips, Alaska wildlife, Iceland midnight sun, and Croatia coast](/blog/july-4th-destinations.svg)

**Classic summer trips:**
- **National parks road trip** — 9 days is perfect for a Yellowstone + Grand Teton loop, or a Pacific Coast Highway drive from San Francisco to LA.
- **Alaska** — Peak season for wildlife, 18+ hours of daylight, and glacier excursions. July is the ideal month.
- **Pacific Northwest (Oregon, Washington)** — Consistently the best weather of the year. Crater Lake, Olympic National Park, the San Juan Islands.

**International:**
- **Iceland** — Midnight sun season. 9 days lets you drive the full Ring Road. July is the warmest month (55–60°F) with nearly 24-hour daylight.
- **Croatia** — Dubrovnik, Split, and the islands (Hvar, Korcula). Early July is warm but slightly less crowded than August. Ferries run frequently.
- **Japan** — Early July is the tail end of rainy season in Tokyo but dry and warm in Hokkaido. Summer festivals (matsuri) begin.

## The Flight Price Trap

![Flight pricing curve from May to July showing sweet spot in May through early June, with prices surging 50-80% by July 4th week](/blog/july-4th-pricing.svg)

July 4th flight prices follow a specific pattern:

- **Outbound:** Flying on Saturday June 27 or Sunday June 28 is significantly cheaper than Wednesday July 1 or Thursday July 2.
- **Return:** Sunday July 5 is the most expensive return day. Monday July 6 or Tuesday July 7 (if you can swing one more PTO day) saves 25–35%.

Book by mid-May for the best domestic rates. International flights to Europe are cheapest 8–10 weeks out.
